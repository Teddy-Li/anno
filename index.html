Instructions

Short Summary

Paraphrase a given computer-generated sequence of questions into an integrated question in natural English. Then rate the original sequence in comparison with your question.

Detailed Instructions

  • You will be given a sequence of questions, these questions are created by machines, and are hard to understand.
  • Each sequence of questions combines to form a query for a certain piece of information from a database.
  • You are asked to provide understandable versions of the same question.
  • Before you start off, please read the following examples carefully. Each example consists of:
    • The machine generated question;
    • An example of a possible response from the database, presented as a table in 'Database Response';
    • An example of a "BAD" English alternative that is still hard to understand;
    • An example of a "GOOD" English alternative that is easy to understand;

Example 1

Topic

department management

Question Sequence

Result 0: Find department's department id, name and everything from management and their corresponding department
Result 1: From Result 0, for each value of department id where the number of management is larger than 1.0 , find department's department id, name and the number of management

Database Response

Department_ID Name COUNT(Everything)
2 Treasury 2

"BAD" Alternative

For each department with management larger than 1, find the department id, name and number of management from all departments.

"GOOD" Alternative

Which department has more than 1 head at a time? List the id, name and the number of heads.

Your Task

  • Your job is to read the machine question sequence and machine response table carefully to understand what it asks for, then rewrite the question in your own words, along the lines of the "GOOD" paraphrases above.
  • You may either ask the question in one sentence or a few sentences as you wish.
  • When you have rewritten the question, please indicate whether the machine question sequence is grammatically valid, whether there are redundant parts and whether it's something that people may actually ask.
  • Lastly, please double check your rewritten question on criteria above and also tell us at how many places did you find our machine question sequence hard to understand.
